Some interesting and cool tech projects

  • 4DReplay

    The Golden State Warriors have partnered with sponsor Zoom to provide "4DReplay" tech in the new Chase Center, using an array of cameras at various levels. Using this tech, referees and broadcast technicians can reproduce an incredible variety of angles in replay. This could potentially be a vast change to the fan experience, as well, because there is talk of a mobile app to use this feature in the future.

  • Glide: "Create an App from a Google Sheet in Minutes"

    I have personally used this Glide app to create a couple of potentially useful apps for my daily life, such as a meal plan tracker and an app for roommates living together in an apartment. This app brings users with a basic knowledge of spreadsheets the ability to put that data and functionality on their smartphones in a simple, functional, and visually appealing way. It does not involve any code for users, and automatically syncs with a selected workbook in their linked Google Drive account.

  • Code Wars

    I found this coding competition website online when searching for projects and programs that encourage kids of all ages to pursue learning and improving programming skills. What I find really interesting about this particular site is its competitive aspect - without having signed up to see what it's like on the inside, it looks like students can participate in high score-based competitions with others, and also join "clans" which compete against other clans. I like this idea, because I do believe that competition is one of the best drivers of self-improvement.
