And here are the 3 projects. I decided to focus on a "game mod" theme, inspired by the novel we are reading for English, Warcross.

  1. Minecraft in a Chest

    2. I find this mod super interesting because it seems so basic and simple yet has so much logic going on in the backend. It allows users to interact with the generated Minecraft world without actually leaving the structure they are in!

  2. Project M

    3. Project M started in response to the gaming community's negative reception to Super Smash Bros Brawl. Of all the titles in the series, Brawl had the least balance among characters in its roster, allowing for a few broken characters to dominate tier lists. Project M takes the basic structure of Brawl and adds its own spin that balances out characters, as well as adds their own items and versions of characters (such as Toad from the Mario franchise).

  3. Garry's Mod

    4. Garry's Mod is the most widely known game mod (at least among PC and Steam users). The game is essentially a massive mod of Valve's game engine. Though it plays like an open-world sandbox title, people have figured out how to use it for animation and other uses!
